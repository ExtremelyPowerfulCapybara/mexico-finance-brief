Fortune Energy
Oil Surges 10% as Tankers Halt Near Strait of Hormuz Following Iran Strikes
Brent crude jumped 10% to roughly $80 a barrel on Sunday after the U.S.-Israel bombing campaign against Iran brought maritime traffic through the Strait of Hormuz — a chokepoint for one-fifth of global oil supply — to a near standstill. Maersk suspended all vessel crossings, the IRGC warned ships against passage and struck three tankers with missiles, and analysts warn prices could hit $100 a barrel if the strait is formally closed. Capital Economics estimates a spike to $100 Brent would add 0.6-0.7 percentage points to global inflation, complicating rate-cutting paths at major central banks.

El Mencho Is Dead, but Mexico's Cartel War Is Just Getting Started — and the Bill Is Coming Due
Mexican special forces killed CJNG kingpin Nemesio "El Mencho" Oseguera last week in the most significant blow to organized crime in Mexico in years, but CJNG retaliated within hours by burning trucks and buildings across 20 states and killing 25 National Guard members. Options-Based 'Overlay Everything' ETFs Swell to $270 Billion as Retail Investors Hedge a Jittery Market
An estimated $170 billion is now invested in synthetic income ETFs using options strategies, with another $100 billion in buffer ETFs designed for downside protection — nearly all of it from retail investors and individual advisors, according to ETF Action's Mike Akins. The trend has accelerated as the S&P 500 has returned less than half a percentage point year-to-date, mega-cap tech balance sheets are deteriorating under AI capex loads, and gold has already gained 20% in 2026. Tidal Financial's Aga Kuplinska describes the current phase as the "overlay everything" era, with issuers applying options income and hedging layers to virtually every asset class, including growth-oriented tech strategies that historically never needed them.
