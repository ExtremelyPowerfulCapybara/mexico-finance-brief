Financial Post Markets
Iran War Sends Oil Surging, Bonds Tumbling, Dollar Higher as Hormuz Threat Bites
West Texas Intermediate settled above $71 as the near-halt of Strait of Hormuz traffic and a Saudi refinery disruption stoked supply fears, while European natural gas soared after Qatar shuttered the world's largest LNG export facility. Ten-year Treasury yields jumped 11 basis points to 4.05% — their biggest single-day advance since April — as inflation fears evaporated Fed rate-cut bets, with markets now pricing the first cut no earlier than September. Gold topped $5,300 and the dollar rose 0.7%, while airlines sank and the S&P 500 clawed back from a 1%-plus intraday loss.

BYD February Sales Plunge 41% Year-on-Year as Chinese Demand Sputters
BYD sold 190,190 vehicles in February, down 41% from a year earlier and 9.5% from January, with the two-month combined figure off 36% at 400,241 units as Lunar New Year disruptions compounded a structural demand softening at home. International expansion — particularly in Latin America and Europe — is now carrying the growth story, with exports of new energy vehicles reaching 100,600 units last month.
