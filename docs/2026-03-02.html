Six Prediction-Market Traders Pocket $1.2M on Iran Strike Bets — Most Within 24 Hours of Attack
Analytics platform Bubblemaps identified six accounts on Polymarket that collectively made approximately $1.2 million betting on U.S. airstrikes against Iran on February 28, with most positions entered within 24 hours of the attack. The timing has triggered immediate speculation about insider knowledge, given that Iranian retaliatory strikes followed across Israel, Dubai, Qatar, Bahrain, and Kuwait, and U.S. and Israeli officials confirmed the death of Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei. Regulators will almost certainly take notice — prediction markets have been eager to prove their legitimacy, and this is exactly the kind of episode that invites congressional scrutiny.

U.S. and Israel Launch Major Combat Operations Against Iran; Khamenei Reported Dead
President Trump confirmed U.S. participation in what Israel's defense minister called a 'pre-emptive strike' against Iran, with Iranian state media subsequently confirming the death of Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei. Retaliatory Iranian strikes were reported in Israel, Dubai, Qatar, Bahrain, and Kuwait, with airport closures and disruptions to vital Gulf shipping lanes following. SpaceX's Bitcoin Hoard Has Lost $235M in Value Ahead of Landmark IPO Filing
SpaceX holds 8,285 BTC across 43 Coinbase Prime custody addresses, worth approximately $544.8 million as of Saturday — down from roughly $780 million in December when bitcoin traded near $92,500, per Arkham Intelligence data. Bloomberg reported the company is targeting a confidential SEC filing as soon as March for a June IPO that would seek a valuation above $1.75 trillion and raise up to $50 billion, eclipsing Saudi Aramco's 2019 record. The S-1 will be the first time SpaceX must publicly account for the bitcoin position, including paper losses in any quarter where BTC declined — an awkward disclosure cadence for a company trying to price the largest IPO in history.

EU Schedules Iran Crisis Meeting for Monday as Bombs Fall on Friday
European Commission President Ursula von der Leyen announced she was convening a special Security College meeting 'on Monday' in response to U.S.-Israeli strikes on Iran — a timeline that drew immediate and sharp criticism given active retaliatory strikes, airport closures, an attack on EU-member Cyprus, and the decapitation of Iran's leadership were already underway. The response crystallizes a long-running critique of European institutional speed relative to the pace of geopolitical crisis. BYD recorded its sharpest monthly sales drop in six years in February, driven by intensifying price competition inside China's domestic EV market even as overseas sales continued to grow. Cardano (ADA) rallied from approximately $0.26 to $0.31 — a 19% gain — to overtake Bitcoin Cash and re-enter the top 10 by market capitalization, with its market cap reaching roughly $10.57 billion versus BCH's $9.72 billion, per CoinMarketCap.
