Fortune Markets
Nvidia Posts Record $68B Quarter, Guides to $78B as Jensen Huang Calls Compute 'Revenues'
Nvidia reported Q4 fiscal 2026 revenue of $68.1 billion, beating guidance by roughly $3 billion and up 73% year-over-year, with GAAP gross margins recovering to 75%. CEO Jensen Huang brushed off AI bubble concerns on the earnings call, arguing that in the new economy 'compute is revenues' — without tokens, cloud providers cannot grow. The company guided Q1 fiscal 2027 revenue to $78 billion and said it has strategically secured supply commitments totaling $95.2 billion, up from $50.3 billion last quarter.

Zelenskyy and Trump Speak as Ukraine Peace Talks Eye Leaders-Level Meeting in March
Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy confirmed a half-hour call with President Trump, joined by envoys Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner, ahead of Geneva talks between Ukraine's Rustem Umerov and US negotiators. The agenda covers a post-war recovery plan, a potential prisoner swap, and groundwork for a trilateral meeting with Russian officials expected in early March. Zelenskyy said a leaders-level summit is 'the only way to resolve all the complex and sensitive issues,' though Moscow and Kyiv remain deadlocked on territory, with the World Bank pegging Ukraine's reconstruction cost at $588 billion.

CJNG Unleashes Wave of Violence Across 20 Mexican States Following El Mencho's Death
The Jalisco New Generation Cartel has turned towns and cities across 20 Mexican states into war zones following the death of drug lord Nemesio 'El Mencho' Oseguera Cervantes after his capture. The BBC embedded with paramedics in Culiacán, Sinaloa — itself still convulsed by internal cartel conflict since El Mayo Zambada's 2024 arrest — where responders said the violence had never been worse or more prolonged. The deteriorating security situation has direct implications for cross-border trade flows, supply chains, and US-Mexico policy negotiations.

US Supreme Court Rules Trump's IEEPA Tariffs Illegal, Though New Levies Already in Place
The US Supreme Court invalidated a broad swath of tariffs imposed under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act, ruling that Congress — not the executive — holds constitutional authority over tariff imposition, and opening the door to potential refunds for affected businesses. Trump responded by immediately enacting a 10% worldwide tariff under Section 122 of the 1974 Trade Act, demonstrating that the ruling is a constraint, not a ceiling. The decision is symbolically significant as a check on executive overreach but offers trading partners only partial relief.

Fashion M&A Deal Value Surged 51.7% in 2025 Despite 6.9% Volume Drop, Outlook Murky
KPMG data shows US consumer sector deal value hit $230 billion in 2025 — up 51.7% — even as total deal count fell 6.9% to 2,559 transactions, underscoring a shift toward fewer, larger bets. Landmark deals included Kimberly-Clark's $48.7 billion Kenvue acquisition and Sycamore Partners' $23.7 billion Walgreens Boots Alliance buyout. Dealmakers at Solomon Partners and Stifel warn that Trump tariff volatility, consumer affordability pressures, and midterm election uncertainty could complicate the 2026 pipeline.

Financial Post Macro
Tecovirimat Fails Phase 3 Trial for Mpox, NEJM Publication Confirms No Benefit
ACTG's STOMP trial, published in the New England Journal of Medicine, found that tecovirimat — widely expected to be an effective mpox treatment — did not reduce lesion resolution time or alleviate pain in a randomized, placebo-controlled study of 412 participants across seven countries. The FDA had approved the drug for smallpox based on animal models, but STOMP is the first rigorous human trial for mpox. The findings, ACTG Chair Joseph Eron said, underscore the critical importance of randomized controlled trials and highlight the absence of proven therapeutics for mpox, which remains a global health concern.

Granite REIT Renews C$250M At-the-Market Equity Program for Acquisitions and Development
Toronto-listed Granite Real Estate Investment Trust (TSX: GRT.UN) has renewed its at-the-market equity distribution program, allowing it to issue up to C$250 million in units through the TSX at prevailing market prices through December 2026. The program, facilitated by BMO Capital Markets, Scotiabank, and TD Securities, is intended to fund acquisitions, development activity, and repayment of credit facility draws. The renewal signals continued appetite for capital-raising in Canadian industrial real estate despite a broader rates-sensitive environment.
