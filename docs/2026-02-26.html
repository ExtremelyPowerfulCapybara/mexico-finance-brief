Indian Exporters Report Surge in US Enquiries After Supreme Court Strikes Down Reciprocal Tariffs
Indian garment, leather, and engineering goods exporters are reporting a revival in American buyer interest following the US Supreme Court's decision to strike down Trump's reciprocal tariff regime, which had imposed levies as high as 50% and pushed Indian exports to the US down 21% year-on-year to $6.6 billion in January 2026. A replacement 10% global surcharge is now in effect for 150 days, but buyers remain confused as Trump's social media posts threaten a 15% rate not yet formally enacted. The refund pipeline for exporters who entered tariff-sharing agreements with US counterparts adds a further layer of complexity to what is, charitably, a chaotic trade policy environment.

Japan Sets 2031 Deadline for Missile Deployment on Island 110km From Taiwan
Japan's Defence Minister Shinjiro Koizumi confirmed Tokyo will deploy surface-to-air missiles on Yonaguni Island — located just 110 kilometres east of Taiwan — by March 2031, the first time a specific timeline has been attached to the plan announced in 2022. The announcement comes as Beijing tightens export restrictions on dozens of Japanese defence-linked firms and its top diplomat accuses Tokyo of seeking to "revive militarism." Prime Minister Takaichi has already suggested Japan could intervene militarily in any attack on Taiwan, a remark that prompted China to discourage nationals from visiting Japan, damaging a tourism sector the economy depends on.

Financial Post Energy
US Fracking Fleets Heading Abroad as Shale Activity Stalls, Tightening Domestic Supply Outlook
Nearly one-fifth of the fracking capacity deployed in the Permian Basin as recently as last year has been shipped to Argentina and Australia by providers including Halliburton, Calfrac, and Liberty Energy, as US shale activity remains depressed and service companies seek margin recovery overseas. Primary Vision estimates up to a quarter of idle US fracking inventory — roughly 8 million horsepower — could be exported within the next two years, a shift that risks straining domestic oilfield service availability if producers attempt to ramp production quickly. The longer-term read: US shale's famed swing-producer status may be structurally slower to activate next cycle than markets currently assume.

UK FCA Selects Revolut and Three Others for Stablecoin Regulatory Sandbox
The Financial Conduct Authority has chosen Revolut, Monee Financial Technologies, ReStabilise, and VVTX from 20 applicants to trial stablecoin services in live market conditions under proposed rules, with testing beginning Q1 2026. The sandbox covers payments, wholesale settlements, and digital asset trading, with findings intended to inform the UK's final stablecoin framework — a process that has dragged through multiple consultation rounds covering issuance, custody, and market abuse. Full regulatory requirements won't take effect until October 2027, which gives the industry a runway but also signals that the UK, despite its ambitions as a crypto hub, is moving deliberately rather than quickly.

Freerepublic.com / The Guardian Macro
Big Four Meatpackers Face Antitrust Scrutiny as Beef Prices Soar and Plants Close
The four companies controlling 85% of US beef processing — Tyson, JBS, Cargill, and National Beef — are under mounting legal and political pressure as consumer prices surge while Tyson simultaneously closed a 3,200-worker plant in Nebraska despite reporting a 6.5% profit increase. Tyson has already settled for $55 million with consumers and $87 million with small retailers over price-fixing allegations, while lawsuits from Kroger, McDonald's, Target, and Sysco pile up. The DOJ inquiry requested by Trump on Truth Social has yet to produce visible action, and the structural point stands: 85% market concentration in a critical food input is a policy problem that predates the current administration by decades.

BusinessLine Trade
India's Textile Exporters Blindsided by 50% Cut to RoDTEP Export Subsidy Rates
India's DGFT has slashed RoDTEP rebate rates and value caps by 50% across all HS lines with immediate effect, shocking manmade fibre and technical textile exporters already navigating global demand uncertainty and recent US tariff disruption. MATEXIL Chairman Shaleen Toshniwal called the move "a big surprise and huge shock," noting the scheme was designed to neutralise embedded taxes and maintain export competitiveness. The RoDTEP review committee was still deliberating when the cut was implemented, meaning exporters had no transition period — an own-goal in trade policy timing that compounds external headwinds.
