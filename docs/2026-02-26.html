New York Post Markets
Nasdaq Rebounds 250 Points as Wall Street Dismisses AI Unemployment 'Doomsday' Report
The Nasdaq surged more than 250 points Wednesday, clawing back losses from a rout triggered by a Citrini Research essay warning that AI could push unemployment above 10% and crash the S&P 500 by 38% by June 2028. Pierre Yared, acting chair of the White House Council of Economic Advisers, called it 'science fiction,' while Citadel Securities argued current data shows no evidence AI will render labour obsolete. Nvidia, the sector's bellwether, reported earnings after the bell — the number that will determine whether this bounce has legs.

ASX 200 Hits Fresh Record at 9,175 as Miners and Tech Lead; Qantas Drops 9.2%
Australia's ASX 200 closed 0.5% higher at a new record of 9,175, buoyed by tech sentiment imported from Wall Street, strong commodity prices lifting Rio Tinto 3.6%, and a 10% surge in Ramsay Healthcare on solid results. BNZ Reverses Mortgage Rate Hike, Cuts 5-Year Fixed Rate 40bps to 5.29%
BNZ has cut its three-, four-, and five-year fixed home loan rates by 10–40 basis points, unwinding a hike it implemented less than a month ago. The reversal follows a more dovish-than-expected Reserve Bank OCR review, which pushed wholesale rates lower after markets had priced in a mid-year hike that never materialised. Westpac made similar cuts last week; the easing cycle in New Zealand mortgage markets is now clearly back in motion.

Modi Addresses Knesset on First Visit to Israel, Reaffirms 'Full Conviction' Support
Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi became the first Indian leader to address Israel's Knesset, declaring India stands with Israel 'firmly, with full conviction,' receiving a standing ovation despite India's domestic public opinion running sharply critical of Israeli operations in Gaza. MrBeast Editor Banned from Kalshi, Reported to CFTC for Insider Trading on Creator's YouTube Activity
Artem Kaptur, an editor for MrBeast, has been banned for two years from prediction market platform Kalshi and fined $20,397 after the company found he used proprietary non-public information to make approximately $4,000 in bets on MrBeast-related event contracts in August and September 2025. Kalshi has reported the matter to the CFTC, marking one of the first significant insider-trading enforcement actions in the fast-growing retail prediction market space. Beast Industries has opened an independent investigation and criticised Kalshi for not sharing its findings before publishing Wednesday's disciplinary notice.

New Zealand Herald Markets
Sky TV Posts NZ$19.3m Half-Year Profit but Warns of Challenging Economy; Neon Subscribers Fall 17%
New Zealand's Sky TV reported a 77% jump in net profit after tax to NZ$19.3m for the six months to December 31, boosted by its acquisition of free-to-air broadcaster Three for $1 and an $8.2m Optus satellite compensation payment. Excluding the Three deal benefits, operating revenue was down 1.3%, and Neon streaming subscribers fell 17% to 215,000. CEO Sophie Moloney flagged that 'economic conditions remain challenging in the near term' but said the company is positioned for earnings growth from FY27.
