Hormuz grey zone: JP Morgan sees oil at $150 if disruption persists through mid-May
The Strait of Hormuz is operating under a selective regime — Iran is authorizing transit for Chinese, Indian, and Pakistani vessels but not Western ones — while the UN Security Council postponed a resolution vote for the second time with no new date set. La Nacion Macro
Brookings: inmigración neta cayó 62% o más en Nueva York, Los Ángeles y Chicago en el último año
Un estudio de la Brookings Institution con datos del Censo de EE.UU. muestra que Nueva York creció apenas 32.000 personas entre julio de 2024 y junio de 2025, frente a 291.000 el año anterior — una caída impulsada principalmente por el colapso de la inmigración neta. Ocho de cada diez condados del país desaceleraron o redujeron su población en el mismo período, con las comunidades fronterizas de Texas, Arizona y California registrando las mayores caídas porcentuales.
La desaceleración demográfica en los principales centros urbanos amenaza directamente la oferta laboral y el consumo en los sectores de servicios y construcción, justo cuando la Fed monitorea el mercado de trabajo como ancla para sus decisiones de tasas.

Brookings: net immigration fell 62% or more in New York, Los Angeles, and Chicago over the past year
A Brookings Institution study using U.S. Census data shows New York grew by just 32,000 people between July 2024 and June 2025, down from 291,000 the prior year — a drop driven primarily by the collapse in net immigration. Eight in ten U.S. counties slowed or reversed population growth in the same period, with border communities in Texas, Arizona, and California recording the steepest percentage declines.
Demographic deceleration in major urban centers directly threatens labor supply and consumption in services and construction, precisely as the Fed monitors the labor market as an anchor for rate decisions.

Milei's 'fiscal innocence' law stalls: private dollar deposits rise just $770 million in 50 days
More than 50 days after a law took effect allowing Argentines to deposit up to $100,000 without declaring its origin, private dollar deposits have risen only $770 million — a figure that pales against the $5.4 billion Argentines bought in foreign currency in July of last year following Caputo's famous 'Buy, champ' dare. Analysts at Ieral-Fundación Mediterránea point to persistent distrust over the promised legal shield and a thin pool of dollar credit borrowers as the main brakes.
