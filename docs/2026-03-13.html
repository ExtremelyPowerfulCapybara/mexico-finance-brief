Gasolina premium y diésel rebasan los 30 pesos por litro en México; el pacto gubernamental no los cubre
El conflicto en Medio Oriente ya se siente en los surtidores mexicanos: el diésel subió 6.1% en 12 días desde el inicio de la guerra, pasando de 26.24 a 27.83 pesos por litro en promedio nacional, con casos aislados que ya superan los 30 pesos. La gasolina regular está protegida por el acuerdo voluntario entre el gobierno y el sector gasolinero, pero el diésel y la premium quedaron fuera, y Pemex trasladó prácticamente el 100% del incremento de la molécula al consumidor final en el primer caso. Con México importando el 60% de la gasolina que consume, el director de GasGas Analytics advierte que el mercado inevitablemente refleja el precio internacional del crudo, el tipo de cambio y los costos logísticos.

Premium gasoline and diesel top 30 pesos per liter in Mexico; government price pact does not cover them
The Middle East conflict is already felt at Mexican gas stations: diesel rose 6.1% in 12 days since the start of the war, moving from 26.24 to 27.83 pesos per liter on the national average, with isolated cases already above 30 pesos. Regular gasoline is protected by the voluntary agreement between the government and the fuel retail sector, but diesel and premium were left out, and Pemex passed virtually 100% of the molecule cost increase to end consumers in the former case. With Mexico importing 60% of the gasoline it consumes, the director of GasGas Analytics warns that the market will inevitably reflect the international crude price, the exchange rate, and domestic logistics costs.

Levy (Brookings): Mexico's stagnation is a productivity problem, not a T-MEC or Middle East problem
Santiago Levy, former IDB Vice President and Brookings senior fellow, was blunt at the IDB Annual Meetings in Asunción: Mexico's 'probably most mediocre growth in Latin America' over 25 years cannot be explained by tariff uncertainty or wars, but by a structural decline in productivity that no export boom has managed to reverse. The IDB projects Mexico will grow just 1.3% in 2026. Levy's diagnosis is damning: productive firms don't grow, unproductive ones survive, and when they die they are replaced by equally unproductive ones.

Argentina: currency band ceiling to exceed $1,700 in April after February inflation prints at 2.9%
INDEC confirmed monthly inflation of 2.9% for February — the second consecutive month at that level — which will push the upper limit of the currency band to approximately $1,703 by end of April, under the CPI-indexation mechanism in place since January. The official wholesale exchange rate closed Thursday at $1,395.89, leaving a 16.5% gap to the current ceiling of $1,626.47 — the widest margin since last July. The widening buffer reduces near-term pressure on the BCRA to intervene, though analyst Christian Buteler notes the ceiling continues to function as an anchor for expectations heading into a month historically marked by the agricultural harvest settlement.

Estafas en EE.UU.: pérdidas reales estimadas en $119,000 millones al año, potenciadas por inteligencia artificial
La Consumer Federation of America estima que las pérdidas anuales por fraude en Estados Unidos ascienden a $119,000 millones, cifra muy superior a los $16,600 millones reportados al FBI en 2024 — una diferencia explicada por el hecho de que el gobierno solo se entera del 14% de estos delitos. Las estafas de inversión, incluyendo criptomonedas y el esquema 'pig butchering', representaron $6,600 millones reportados en 2024, pero la CFA estima el monto real en $46,600 millones. El factor acelerador es la inteligencia artificial, que permite a los estafadores operar con menos mano de obra y mayor precisión, comprando bases de datos con perfiles de víctimas potenciales por precios irrisorios.

Fraud in the US: real losses estimated at $119 billion per year, accelerated by artificial intelligence
The Consumer Federation of America estimates annual fraud losses in the United States at $119 billion, far above the $16.6 billion reported to the FBI in 2024 — a gap explained by the fact that the government learns of only 14% of such crimes. Investment scams, including cryptocurrency fraud and 'pig butchering' schemes, accounted for $6.6 billion in reported losses in 2024, but the CFA estimates the true figure at $46.6 billion. The accelerant is artificial intelligence, which allows fraudsters to operate with less manpower and greater precision, purchasing databases of potential victims at negligible cost.
