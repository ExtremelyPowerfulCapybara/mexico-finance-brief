ABC News Macro
US consumer confidence edges up to 91.2 in February but recession warning gauge stays below 80 for 13th straight month
The Conference Board's consumer confidence index rose to 91.2 from a revised 89 in January, offering modest relief after a sharp drop. However, the expectations sub-index — a recession leading indicator — rose only to 72, still well below the critical 80 threshold for the 13th consecutive month. Trade and politics are top of mind for respondents, while the labor market added just 584,000 jobs in all of 2025, roughly one-quarter of 2024's pace.

Russia's war-remade economy is sowing the seeds of a long-term structural slump
A wave of analysis — from the NYT to Reuters Breakingviews to The Economist — converges on the same conclusion: Russia's pivot to a war footing has inflated short-term activity while gutting long-term productive capacity. Elevated military spending is crowding out investment, labor shortages are deepening, and inflation is running hot. The longer the war continues, the steeper the eventual economic unwind.

The multifamily REIT — formerly Mack-Cali Realty — has agreed to be acquired by an Affinius Capital and Vista Hill Partners consortium in an all-cash deal expected to close in Q2 2026, pending shareholder approval. Bear case on RXO: Truck broker may be near zero cash, raising solvency concerns
Analyst Keith Dalrymple via DF Research argues RXO, Inc. faces a severe liquidity crisis after Q4 2025, estimating cash balances have likely hit zero following negative free cash flow of roughly $25 million. Gross margins are estimated to have declined 130–225 basis points sequentially as spot freight rates surged faster than RXO could reprice contracts, with EBITDA falling well short of management's $20–30 million guidance. Accounting opacity — including the abrupt departure of the Chief Accounting Officer — adds another layer of concern to an already fragile balance sheet.

BusinessLine Macro
Indian banks face narrowing margins as deposits migrate to mutual funds, equities, and gold
A structural shift is underway in Indian banking as digitally savvy retail depositors move savings into higher-yielding alternatives, forcing banks to replace cheap retail deposits with expensive wholesale funding at rates of 7.5% or more, compressing net interest margins. Physical cash withdrawals surged to ₹4.4 trillion in the 14 months to January 2026 — three times the prior year's pace — and RBI dollar-selling operations are permanently removing rupee liquidity. The asset-liability mismatch risk is prompting calls for a fundamental recalibration of the Indian banking business model.
