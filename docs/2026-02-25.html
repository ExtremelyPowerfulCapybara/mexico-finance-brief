Insurance Journal Mexico
AM Best warns Mexico cartel violence threatens tourism-dependent economy and insurance segment
Cartel violence triggered by the killing of Jalisco New Generation Cartel leader 'El Mencho' disrupted Puerto Vallarta and Guadalajara airports over the weekend, rattling a tourism sector that accounts for roughly 10% of Mexico's GDP. AM Best, which already holds a negative outlook on Mexico's insurance segment, warns that continued unrest compounds existing headwinds including a slowing economy and a VAT rule change that could wipe out 40% of insurers' expected 2025 net profits. Reinsurers had been returning to the market on price; that calculus may now shift.

Samsung hits record 200,000 won on HBM4 shipments; stock up 60% year-to-date
Samsung Electronics closed at an all-time high of 200,000 won on February 24, up 3.63% on the day and more than 60% since January 1, driven by the commencement of commercial HBM4 chip shipments delivering 11.7 Gbps transfer speeds — a 22% improvement over its HBM3E predecessor. Q4 2025 operating profit tripled year-over-year to a record 20.1 trillion won, with the semiconductor division alone generating 16.4 trillion won in operating income. HBM sales are forecast to more than triple in 2026 as Samsung aggressively targets Nvidia-powered AI infrastructure customers where rival SK Hynix has dominated.

Waymo expands robotaxi service to 10 U.S. markets, targets 1 million weekly trips by end of 2026
Waymo announced Tuesday it will deploy robotaxis in Dallas, Houston, San Antonio, and Orlando, bringing its total footprint to 10 major U.S. metropolitan markets and more than 400,000 weekly trips currently across its existing six cities.
