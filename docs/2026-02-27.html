First U.S. Primary Aluminum Plant in 45 Years Breaks Ground in Oklahoma, Fueled by Tariff Policy
Emirates Global Aluminum and Century Aluminum announced a new $multi-billion smeltery in Inola, Oklahoma — the first primary aluminum production facility built in the U.S. since 1980 — projected to produce 750,000 tons annually and more than double current domestic output. The project, requiring 1.2 gigawatts of continuous power and expected to generate 5,000 jobs, is a direct byproduct of the 50% tariff on steel, aluminum, and copper that survived the Supreme Court's broader tariff rollback in February. Whether this is industrial policy working or simply expensive reshoring math remains the open question.

Bipartisan Bill Seeks to Shield Crypto Developers from Money Transmission Liability After Tornado Cash Convictions
Representatives Fitzgerald, Cline, and Lofgren introduced the Promoting Innovation in Blockchain Development Act of 2026, targeting 18 U.S.C. The bill's bipartisan framing signals that developer liability has become a live political fault line, not just an industry grievance.
