A weekend post by Citrini Research — framed as a fictional 2028 research memo titled 'The 2028 Global Intelligence Crisis' — helped trigger a $200 billion tech and software selloff on Monday, with closing figures at multi-year lows. The scenario described an AI-saturated economy where companies fire white-collar workers, reinvest in AI, improve AI further, and then repeat until the labor market collapses. Citrini's thesis — that narratives drive markets more than any other factor — proved itself rather efficiently.
