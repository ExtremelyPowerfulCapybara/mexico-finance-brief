Nvidia Falls 5% Post-Earnings as Wall Street Questions AI Demand Durability

The issue isn't the numbers; it's what wasn't said: the forward guidance excludes potential China revenue, and management offered little on when the AI capex supercycle actually translates into returns for enterprise customers. Bitcoin's Decline Is Capitulation, Not Conspiracy — Bitwise CIO
Speculation pinning Bitcoin's slide on Jane Street, Binance, and unnamed macro funds has been doing the rounds, but Bitwise CIO Matt Hougan's read is blunter: it's a textbook mid-cycle deleveraging, months in the making. The roughly 45% peak-to-trough drawdown shows the hallmarks of forced liquidation and margin cascade rather than coordinated manipulation — sharp moves, heavy volume near the lows, and a prior distribution phase visible well before February's acceleration. Selling pressure appears to be easing, though the structural damage to leveraged positioning is done.

30-Year Mortgage Rate Drops Below 6% for First Time Since September 2022
Freddie Mac put the benchmark 30-year fixed rate at 5.98% this week, its third consecutive decline and the lowest print in over three years. The 10-year Treasury yield sits at 4.02%, providing the gravitational pull. Despite the psychological milestone, existing home sales just posted their largest monthly drop in nearly four years — a reminder that rate relief alone cannot fix a market where nearly 69% of outstanding mortgages carry rates at or below 5%.

First U.S. Primary Aluminum Smelter Since 1980 to Break Ground in Oklahoma
Emirates Global Aluminum and Century Aluminum are building a 750,000-ton annual capacity smelter in Inola, Oklahoma — the first new primary aluminum plant in the U.S. in 45 years — citing Trump's 50% tariff on the metal as a key enabling condition. The Supreme Court struck down Trump's broader tariff authority on February 20 but left the metals duties intact, which now appear to be actively reshaping industrial supply chains. Construction starts later this year; production is expected by end of decade.

Bipartisan Bill Seeks to Shield DeFi Developers from Money Transmission Liability
Representatives Fitzgerald, Cline, and Lofgren introduced the Promoting Innovation in Blockchain Development Act of 2026, targeting 18 U.S.C. Section 1960 — the statute used to prosecute Tornado Cash co-founders Roman Storm and Roman Semenov and the Samourai Wallet team. The bill would narrow the law's scope to entities with actual custody of user funds, explicitly carving out open-source developers. It arrives as Congress works through broader digital asset market structure legislation, and the stakes are high: Storm was convicted in August 2025, Samourai's leadership entered guilty pleas by November.
