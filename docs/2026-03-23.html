Argentina's fiscal asymmetry: grain futures taxed up to 35% while Bitcoin pays just 15%
A tax analysis reveals that derivative instruments on grains — futures and options on soybeans, corn, and wheat — are excluded from Article 98 of Argentina's Income Tax Law, which sets a flat 15% rate for stocks, bonds, and digital currencies including crypto assets. In practice, an agricultural producer hedging prices in futures markets can be taxed up to 35% with additional restrictions on loss offsets, while an equivalent gain in Bitcoin is taxed at 15%. The reform is urgent: the asymmetry actively discourages risk management in the sector that generates the vast majority of the country's foreign exchange earnings.
