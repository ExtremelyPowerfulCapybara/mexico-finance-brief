PIMCO's Ivascyn Cuts Credit, Hoards Cash as Middle East War Upends Bond Market Playbook
PIMCO CIO Daniel Ivascyn had already trimmed corporate credit and built cash buffers to exploit dislocations when the US-Israeli attack on Iran broke out, adding a new inflation shock to an already complicated setup. Instead of rallying as a safe haven, Treasuries sold off as crude prices surged — 10-year yields climbed as high as 4.21% — while a surprise drop in US payrolls simultaneously raised the specter of stagflation. UBS chief strategist Bhanu Baweja warned bluntly: 'If the oil problem persists, it'll become a growth problem.'

BusinessLine Markets
Crude Shock Threatens Indian FMCG Margins as Packaging and Freight Costs Climb
If crude holds above $90 per barrel, Indian FMCG companies face 100–150 basis points of gross margin pressure and likely price hikes of 1–3% over the next two quarters, according to LKP Securities analyst Sandeep Abhange. Packaging — PET bottles, HDPE containers, laminated wrappers — accounts for 12–18% of COGS and is directly indexed to crude derivatives, while logistics adds another 6–8% of sales exposure. Mid-tier players with limited pricing power are most exposed, and potential Strait of Hormuz freight disruptions could amplify the hit further.

Malaysia's Zafrul: Government Monitoring Oil Price Volatility 'Every Hour' Amid Middle East Conflict
Senior Political Advisor to Malaysia's Prime Minister, Tengku Zafrul Abdul Aziz, said Kuala Lumpur is tracking geopolitical developments in real time, with particular concern for petrol subsidy implications if crude remains elevated. He noted oil has previously reached $100 per barrel and stressed that Malaysia's Finance and Economy ministries have contingency plans in place. As an open-economy, neutral state with significant Middle East trade ties, Malaysia's exposure to energy price swings is both fiscal and diplomatic.

CNBC Macro
AI-Driven White-Collar Recession Fuels Surge in Skilled Trades Enrollment
As large employers accelerate layoffs and AI erodes entry-level white-collar roles, career experts and enrollment data point to a structural rotation toward skilled trades — fields that require physical presence and cannot be offshored or automated. Electricians earn a median $1,376 per week — 14% above the national median — and the sector faces a net deficit of roughly 80,000 workers as 20,000 union electricians retire annually with insufficient replacements. Citrini Research has described the dynamic as a 'negative feedback loop with no natural brake,' while Monster career expert Vicki Salemi calls the trades 'the underdog and AI-proof.'

India Forms 17-Member Committee to Overhaul SEZ Policy Under 'SEZ 2.0' Blueprint
New Delhi has constituted a 17-member advisory body to design a structural overhaul of its Special Economic Zone framework, tasked with evaluating the SEZ Act's effectiveness, its interaction with export support programs, and recent policy adaptations. The initiative, branded SEZ 2.0, reflects broader efforts to sharpen India's export competitiveness at a moment when global trade routes and tariff regimes are in flux.
