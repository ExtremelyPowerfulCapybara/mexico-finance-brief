BusinessLine Trade
India awaits operational details on U.S. DFC maritime reinsurance facility as Gulf shipping risk escalates
India is holding for clarity from the U.S. Treasury on the DFC's $20 billion rolling maritime reinsurance facility announced March 6 by DFC CEO Ben Black and Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent, including which vessels qualify and at what insured amounts. The facility covers hull, machinery, and cargo — including war risk — for vessels transiting the Strait of Hormuz, and is being coordinated directly with CENTCOM. Until vessel selection criteria are published, India's oil ministry and international shippers are operating in a guidance vacuum during the most acute Gulf disruption in years.

Military Times Politics
Pentagon and FAA agree to anti-drone laser tests in New Mexico after two unauthorized airspace closures over Texas
The Pentagon and FAA announced joint anti-drone laser testing at White Sands Missile Range following two February incidents in which military laser deployment — including one that accidentally shot down a CBP drone — forced sudden airspace closures over El Paso and Fort Hancock without FAA notification. The coordination breakdown drew congressional scrutiny, with Sen. Tammy Duckworth calling for an independent investigation. The incidents expose a structural gap in interagency protocol as counter-drone operations along the southern border intensify.

Enterprise AI 'deployment gap' widens as 88% of companies report AI use but most remain stuck in pilot stage
Infosys co-founder Nandan Nilekani described a widening gap between AI model capability and real-world enterprise deployment at the company's Investors AI Day, a view echoed by Forrester analyst Biswajeet Mahapatra, who noted that very few firms can connect AI initiatives to P&L outcomes.
