Yahoo Finance / AP Energy
Canada Announces Cuba Aid Package as U.S. Oil Embargo Deepens Energy Crisis
Canada's Foreign Minister Anita Anand confirmed Monday that Ottawa is preparing an aid package for Cuba, which is facing severe blackouts and fuel shortages after the U.S. oil embargo cut off Venezuelan and Mexican supply. Cuba produces only 40% of its required fuel, and Air Canada has already suspended flights due to aviation fuel shortages. Mexico previously sent two Navy ships with roughly 2,300 tons of humanitarian goods; Canada is now following suit, putting both countries in direct friction with Washington's embargo posture.

Business Insider Politics
Trump Administration Confirms $1.7 Trillion Federal Student Loan Portfolio Sale Is Under Active Consideration
The Department of Education's Undersecretary Nicholas Kent confirmed in a letter to Sen. Elizabeth Warren that the administration has held meetings with finance executives about selling all or part of the $1.7 trillion federal student loan portfolio. The department stopped short of announcing any final decision, but this marks the first official public acknowledgment of the discussions first reported by Politico in October. Private lenders are already positioning for an influx of borrowers, raising serious questions about which income-driven repayment protections would survive a transfer to private hands.

Supreme Court Tariff Ruling Creates Strategic Uncertainty for India's Export Sector
India's Economic Times flags that the U.S. Supreme Court's tariff ruling introduces fresh instability for Indian exporters who had been navigating Trump-era levies. The ruling forces a recalibration of what trade exposure actually looks like for emerging market economies that had been factoring in a specific tariff regime. The follow-on threat of a 15% global tariff compounds the uncertainty for policymakers in New Delhi.

PCMag Politics
SpaceX Launches Official Starlink Retail Sales in Venezuela, Adds Demand Surcharge Starting March 6
SpaceX has begun selling Starlink dishes through three official Venezuelan retailers — Daka, Multimax, and Soytechno — with the standard dish priced at $469. Free access ended February 3, and starting March 6, new residential activations will carry a one-time demand surcharge that has reached $1,500 in parts of the U.S. and $1,000 in Mexico City. The commercialization push follows the U.S. military intervention that removed Nicolás Maduro in January, making Venezuela an active frontier for both geopolitics and satellite broadband expansion.
